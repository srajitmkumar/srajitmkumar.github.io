about

I am a PhD candidate at the South Asia Institute, Heidelberg University, supervised by Prof. Dr. Kama Maclean and funded by a DAAD PhD scholarship. I read for my BA in History at Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi (2020), and for my MA in History at the University of Delhi (2022).

research

My dissertation, Time, (Non)Work and Political Consciousness: The Kanpur Working Class, 1914–47, examines how labourers from the agrarian United Provinces adapted to industrial employment in Kanpur — in particular, how their perception and experience of time shifted during this transition, and the political consequences of that shift.

More broadly, I am interested in labour history, the partition of India, communalism, eighteenth-century South Asia, and the urban history of Delhi.

    Colloquium, South Asia Institute, Heidelberg University
